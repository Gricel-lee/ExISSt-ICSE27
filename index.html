About

International Workshop on Engineering Explainability for Intelligent Software Systems (ExISSt) brings together researchers and practitioners working with intelligent software systems to discuss exchange of ideas, strengthen links between communities, and work towards establishing explainability throughout the software engineering lifecycle.

As intelligent software systems grow in autonomy and complexity, their decisions become increasingly opaque — particularly in safety-critical domains such as transportation, healthcare, and robotics, where incomprehensible behavior can undermine safe operation, regulatory compliance, and user acceptance. Explainability has been identified as a means to achieve ethics, fairness, and transparency, and with regulatory frameworks such as the EU AI Act, it is evolving from a desirable property into a legal requirement. Yet research on systematic explainability engineering remains scarce and fragmented across different communities.

ExISSt aims to provide a dedicated forum to consolidate these efforts within the software engineering community. The workshop pursues three objectives: advancing research on systematic engineering for explainable systems and software, fostering community building, and promoting interdisciplinary exchange. By bringing together researchers from software engineering, human-computer interaction, philosophy, psychology, and cyber-physical systems, the workshop works towards identifying and tackling open research problems in the area of explainability as a first-class citizen of the software engineering process.

Call for Contributions

Workshop contributions can be full research papers and short papers.

  • Research papers (max. 8 pages with references). These papers should describe ongoing research or preliminary results aligned with the workshop's topics of interest. They should already have a proof of concept or a distinct vision of what the research aims at. They could be improvements of existing approaches, as well as empirical studies, and experience reports (e.g., of applied work in the industry).
  • Short papers (max. 5 pages with references). These papers serve to foster discussion on hot, relevant topics in the field. They could outline rough, but plausible visions regarding the future of explainability, as well as problem statements explaining challenges with explainability in industrial settings.

In both cases, papers will be peer-reviewed and accepted papers will be published in the IEEE workshop proceedings. Submissions should clearly describe how they relate to the workshop scope and objectives. The submission deadline is To be defined.

Scope

The scope of ExISSt is intentionally broad, reflecting both the diversity of software engineering disciplines and the growing relevance of explainability across intelligent systems. Topics span the full software engineering lifecycle — from requirements and design to implementation, testing, and maintenance — and address explainability from theoretical, methodological, and applied perspectives. Contributions from adjacent fields such as human-computer interaction, philosophy, psychology, and law are equally welcome.

Explainability Foundations / Engineering Explainable Systems

  • Defining and measuring explainability
  • Stakeholders and contexts considerations for explainability
  • Goals and desiderata for explanations
  • Explainability for developers vs. users
  • Creating explanations: models, languages, design, algorithms
  • Design processes for explainability
  • Quality aspects and testing
  • Quantifying and reducing costs and resources
  • AI-based explainable software engineering
